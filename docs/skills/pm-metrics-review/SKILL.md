# Metrics Review

When to use: you have a dashboard, a data export, or a weekly number and need to know what actually changed, why it matters, and what to do about it, not just restate the figures.

METHOD
1. Anchor on the goal: which metric maps to the outcome you care about (activation, retention, revenue, engagement)? Separate the north-star from supporting and guardrail metrics.
2. Establish baseline and trend: compare against prior period, same period last cycle, and target. Report direction, magnitude, and whether the change is outside normal noise.
3. Decompose movement: split by segment, cohort, surface, or funnel step to locate where the change originates. A flat top line often hides opposing sub-trends.
4. Form hypotheses for each notable move; tie to known releases, seasonality, or external events. Label causal claims as hypotheses unless you have a clean before/after.
5. Rank insights by impact and confidence. Drop the ones that are interesting but not actionable.
6. Recommend concrete next steps: investigate, ship, or hold, each with an owner.

OUTPUT FORMAT
- Headline: the one thing leadership must know.
- Metric table: value, delta vs prior, delta vs target, trend arrow.
- Key movements with likely drivers (marked hypothesis where unproven).
- Watch list: metrics trending toward trouble.
- Recommended actions with owner and expected effect.
State data window and any quality caveats up front.
