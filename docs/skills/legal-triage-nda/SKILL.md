## When to use
Use this when an NDA arrives and someone needs a fast, consistent read: can we sign as-is, does it need light negotiation, or must it go to counsel. It applies a standard rubric so NDAs do not silently pile up or get signed with hidden traps.

## METHOD
1. **Capture the basics.** Parties, one-way vs. mutual, purpose, and the disclosing/receiving direction that matters to us.
2. **Check the core terms** against standard: definition of confidential information, permitted use, exclusions (public, independently developed, already known), term and survival, return/destruction, and governing law.
3. **Scan for red flags:** non-competes or non-solicits hidden inside, IP assignment, indemnification, unlimited or perpetual duration, unusually broad "confidential" definitions, one-sided obligations on us, residuals clauses, or foreign jurisdiction.
4. **Classify.** GREEN: standard, mutual, reasonable term — sign as-is. YELLOW: minor deviations negotiable with playbook edits. RED: red-flag term, IP/indemnity/non-compete, or high-stakes counterparty — send to counsel.
5. **Recommend action** matching the color, with specific edits for YELLOW.
6. **Note the deadline** and who owns the next step.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Parties / type / purpose.**
- **Classification:** GREEN / YELLOW / RED.
- **Term check table:** term, standard, this NDA, ok?
- **Red flags** (if any).
- **Recommended action:** sign / negotiate (edits) / escalate.
- **Owner & deadline.**
