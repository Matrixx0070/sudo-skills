## When to use

Reach for this when a specific role asks for (or allows) a cover letter and you want to connect your track record to *this* company's problem. Best used after car-resume so the letter amplifies your two or three strongest proof points rather than repeating the full list.

**Not for:** the employer side. Writing the job posting or offer letter is hr-draft-offer; defining role requirements is hr-interview-prep. This skill makes *your* case to a hiring manager.

## Method

1. **Research the company** — find one concrete signal: a recent launch, funding, stated priority, or problem the role clearly exists to solve. Decision point: if you find nothing specific, keep researching — a generic letter is worse than none.
2. **Pick your throughline** — choose the single strongest reason you fit *this* role, and the two or three achievements that prove it.
3. **Open with a hook** — name the role and lead with your specific relevance or a striking result; skip "I am writing to apply for."
4. **Prove it in the body** — 1-2 short paragraphs mapping your quantified wins to the role's needs, using the company signal to show you understand their context.
5. **Show fit both ways** — one line on why *this* company (mission, product, problem) that isn't flattery.
6. **Close with a call to action** — restate fit in one line, state availability, thank them. Decision point: keep the whole letter under one page (~250-350 words); if longer, cut, don't shrink font.

## Example

Opening for a fintech Series B: "Your November launch of instant payouts is exactly the reliability problem I spent three years solving — I cut payment-failure rates from 4.1% to 0.6% at [Company] while scaling to 2M daily transactions." It names a real company event, leads with a metric, and states relevance in one sentence.

## Pitfalls

- Restating the resume line-by-line instead of telling the story behind two or three wins.
- Generic praise ("I've always admired your innovative culture") that could apply to any company.
- Writing about what the job would do for you instead of what you would do for them.
- Padding to fill a page — tight and specific beats long and vague.

## Output format

```
Header: name | contact | date | hiring manager/team (named if known)
Hook (1-2 sentences): role + strongest relevance or result
Company signal: the specific thing you researched
Body 1: proof point #1 mapped to a role need (with metric)
Body 2: proof point #2 + why-this-company (genuine, specific)
Close: one-line fit restatement + availability + thanks
Word count: 250-350
```
