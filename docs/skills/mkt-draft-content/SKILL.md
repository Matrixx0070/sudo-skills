When to use this skill when you need a first draft of a specific asset for a specific channel and want it formatted correctly, on-message, and search-aware from the start.

METHOD

1. Confirm the brief. Establish channel (blog/social/email/landing/press), audience, primary goal, single key message, desired CTA, tone, and length. Fill gaps with labeled assumptions.
2. Identify the primary keyword and 2-3 secondary terms for search-relevant channels (blog, landing, press).
3. Draft the hook first. Write a headline plus 2 alternates; open with the reader's problem or a concrete benefit, never with throat-clearing.
4. Structure for the channel: blog uses scannable H2/H3 sections; social respects platform length and native tone; email uses subject + preview + short body + one CTA; landing uses hero, benefits, social proof, CTA; press uses inverted-pyramid with dateline and boilerplate.
5. Apply SEO where relevant: keyword in title, first 100 words, one subhead; meta description under 155 chars; descriptive links; alt text notes for images.
6. End with a clear, single primary CTA.
7. Self-check against goal, message, and tone before delivering.

OUTPUT FORMAT

- Channel and target keyword(s).
- Headline plus 2 alternates.
- The full draft in correct channel formatting.
- For SEO channels: suggested meta title and meta description.
- CTA and any A/B variant.
- Notes: assumptions made and image/alt-text placeholders.
