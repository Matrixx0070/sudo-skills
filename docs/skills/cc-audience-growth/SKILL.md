## When to use

Use this when a creator needs a concrete growth plan — not tactics in isolation, but positioning, content pillars, a realistic cadence, and the metrics that prove it is working. Good at any stage from 0 to mid-size.

**Not for:** writing individual scripts/titles (use the cc-* content skills), paid-ads media plans, or one-off "how do I go viral" answers.

## Method

1. Define the niche and the viewer transformation. Who is this for and what changes for them? Decision point: if the niche is "everyone," pick a wedge — a specific sub-audience to win first.
2. Set positioning: the one sentence that says why this channel over the alternatives.
3. Choose 3-4 content pillars — repeatable themes that serve the niche and give you a near-infinite idea supply.
4. Pick a sustainable cadence and format mix. Decision point: fewer high-quality posts beat a burnout schedule; match cadence to the creator's real capacity.
5. Design the discovery engine: which platform/format drives new reach (usually short-form or search) vs. which deepens loyalty (long-form, email). Route new viewers toward the owned channel (email).
6. Set a 90-day milestone with 2-3 leading metrics (not just subscriber count): impressions, click-through, average view duration, and email signups.
7. Schedule a monthly review to double down on top performers and cut dead pillars.

## Example

Niche: home-cooking for people who "can't cook." Wedge: absolute beginners intimidated by recipes. Positioning: "cooking explained like you've never held a knife." Pillars: (1) technique basics, (2) 5-ingredient meals, (3) fixing common mistakes, (4) grocery/tool guides. Cadence: 3 Shorts + 1 long-form weekly, weekly email. Engine: Shorts for discovery → long-form for depth → email for retention. 90-day milestone: 25k views/mo, 6% CTR, 1k email subs. Review: monthly, cut the lowest pillar.

## Pitfalls

- Chasing every trend with no niche, so the algorithm and audience never learn what you are.
- Vanity metrics (follower count) over leading indicators (CTR, view duration, signups).
- An unsustainable cadence that causes a burnout gap and a cold restart.
- No owned channel (email), leaving all reach rented from one algorithm.

## Output format

```
NICHE + WEDGE: <...>
VIEWER TRANSFORMATION: <before -> after>
POSITIONING (1 sentence): <...>

CONTENT PILLARS (3-4):
1. <pillar> — <example ideas>
...

CADENCE: <formats x frequency, matched to capacity>
DISCOVERY ENGINE: <discovery format> -> <depth format> -> <owned: email>

90-DAY MILESTONE: <goal>
LEADING METRICS: <3 metrics + targets>
MONTHLY REVIEW: double down on <...>, cut <...>
```
