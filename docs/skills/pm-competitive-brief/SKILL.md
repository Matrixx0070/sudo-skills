# Competitive Brief

When to use: you are entering a crowded market, prepping a sales team, or deciding where to place bets against named rivals. Use this to turn scattered intel into a decision-grade brief instead of a feature grid nobody reads.

METHOD
1. Frame the job: name the 2-5 competitors that actually show up in deals, and state the buyer and use case you are comparing on. Ignore vendors who never appear.
2. Gather evidence per competitor: pricing, positioning, target segment, standout capabilities, recent releases, and public weaknesses. Cite sources; mark anything inferred as ASSUMPTION.
3. Score on the 5-7 dimensions your buyer cares about (not every feature). Use a simple Strong / Parity / Weak rating so gaps are visible at a glance.
4. Find the wedge: where are you genuinely better, where are they, and where is the market underserved by all of you? The last is your best differentiation.
5. Write battlecards: one per competitor with "why we win," "why we lose," landmines to plant, and objection-handling responses.
6. End with 3 strategic implications and recommended actions with owners.

OUTPUT FORMAT
- Executive summary (3-5 bullets, the "so what").
- Comparison matrix (dimensions x competitors, rated).
- Battlecard per competitor.
- Differentiation narrative (2-3 sentences a rep can say out loud).
- Strategic implications and next actions.
Flag stale or unverified intel explicitly.
