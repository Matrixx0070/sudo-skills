# Sales Account Research

## When to use
Use this when you are handed an account or contact name and need a fast, decision-grade profile before outreach, a discovery call, or a QBR. The goal is signals that change what you say next — triggers, stack, people, and a pain hypothesis.

**Not for:** generic company overviews or investor-style deep dives (that's a Wikipedia dump); post-call recaps (use sales-call-summary); competitor teardowns (use sales-competitive-intel).

## Method
1. **Frame the goal.** Confirm the product you sell and the deal stage. Scope everything to "what would make this account buy."
2. **Firmographics.** Capture size, revenue band, industry, HQ/regions, funding stage, ownership. Note what tier this puts them in.
3. **Trigger events.** Hunt the last 6-12 months: funding, leadership hires, M&A, layoffs, launches, earnings commentary, regulatory pressure. **Decision point:** no trigger found → widen to industry-level pressures rather than inventing one.
4. **Tech and buying signals.** Identify current stack, competitors in use, and job postings that reveal initiatives or pain.
5. **People.** Map the likely economic buyer, champion, and blocker. For a named person: role tenure, prior companies, public posts, mutual connections.
6. **Fit and hypothesis.** State one specific pain you solve and a value hypothesis tied to a trigger. **Decision point:** if fit is weak, say so and recommend disqualify rather than force a play.
7. **Flag gaps.** Mark anything unverified rather than inventing it.

## Example
Input: "Research Acme Logistics; we sell freight-visibility software; top-of-funnel."
Output snapshot: "Mid-market 3PL, ~800 staff, just raised a $40M Series C (Mar 2026) earmarked for tech modernization → they're buying software now. New VP Ops from a company that ran real-time tracking → warm to your category. Hiring 3 'supply chain data' roles → visibility gap is real. Play: reference the Series C mandate and open with the tracking-gap hypothesis to the new VP Ops."

## Pitfalls
- **Fact-dumping.** Firmographics without a "so what" is noise. Every fact earns its place by changing the outreach.
- **Inventing triggers.** A vague "they're probably growing" is worse than none. Cite or omit.
- **Ignoring the person.** Company research with no named human leaves you with nobody to call.
- **Stale signals.** A 3-year-old funding round is not a reason to reach out now.

## Output format
```
Snapshot: <one-line who-they-are>
Firmographics:
  - <size / revenue / industry / HQ / funding / ownership>
Trigger events (most recent first):
  - <date> — <event> — so what: <why it matters now>
Key people:
  - <name> — <role> — angle: <how to approach>
Pain hypothesis: <2-3 sentences tied to a trigger>
Recommended plays:
  1. <concrete next action>
  2. <concrete next action>
  3. <concrete next action>
Confidence & gaps: <verified vs. assumed>
```
