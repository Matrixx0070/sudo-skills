# Sales Account Research

## When to use
Use this when you are handed an account or contact name and need a fast, decision-grade profile before outreach, a call, or a QBR — not a Wikipedia dump, but signals that change what you say next.

## METHOD
1. **Frame the goal.** Confirm the product you sell and the deal stage. Research is scoped to "what would make this account buy."
2. **Firmographics.** Capture size, revenue band, industry, HQ/regions, funding stage, and ownership. Note what tier this puts them in.
3. **Trigger events.** Hunt the last 6-12 months: funding, leadership hires, M&A, layoffs, product launches, earnings commentary, regulatory pressure. Each trigger is a reason to reach out.
4. **Tech and buying signals.** Identify current stack, competitors they use, and job postings that reveal initiatives or pain.
5. **People.** Map the likely economic buyer, champion, and blocker. For a named person: role tenure, prior companies, public posts, mutual connections.
6. **Fit and hypothesis.** State a specific pain you can solve and the value hypothesis tied to a trigger.
7. **Flag gaps.** Mark anything unverified rather than inventing it.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Snapshot:** one-line who-they-are.
- **Firmographics:** bulleted facts.
- **Trigger events:** dated, most-recent first, each with a "so what."
- **Key people:** name, role, angle.
- **Pain hypothesis:** 2-3 sentences.
- **Recommended plays:** 3 concrete next actions.
- **Confidence and gaps:** what is verified vs. assumed.
