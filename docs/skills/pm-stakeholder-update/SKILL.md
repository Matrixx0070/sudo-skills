# Stakeholder Update

When to use: you need to communicate progress, status, or a decision to a specific audience and want it framed for what they care about, not one generic memo forwarded to everyone.

METHOD
1. Identify the audience and their stake: executives want outcomes, risk, and asks; engineering wants scope, dependencies, and tradeoffs; customers want value, timing, and what it means for them. Tailor depth and vocabulary accordingly.
2. Lead with the headline: the single most important thing this reader needs to know, stated in the first line.
3. Report status honestly: on-track / at-risk / off-track, with the reason. Never bury bad news; surface it early with a plan.
4. Give evidence proportional to the audience: metrics and dates for execs, technical detail for engineers, concrete benefits for customers.
5. State what you need: decisions, resources, or unblocking, each with an owner and a deadline. An update with no ask is often just noise.
6. Keep it scannable and short; respect the reader's time.

OUTPUT FORMAT
- Headline (one line).
- Status: on-track / at-risk / off-track, with reason.
- What shipped / what's next (audience-appropriate detail).
- Risks and mitigations.
- Asks: decision or help needed, owner, by when.
Adjust tone and length to the named audience; state which audience this version targets.
