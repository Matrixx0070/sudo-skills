# Tax Prep

## When to use
Use this ahead of a quarterly estimated-tax deadline or at year-end to organize what the owner needs to file or hand to their accountant — so nothing is missed and the accountant's time isn't wasted on cleanup.

## METHOD
1. **Set the job.** State whether this is a quarterly estimate or year-end (1099s, annual packet) and the period/deadline.
2. **Summarize income and expenses.** Pull revenue and deductible expenses by category from the books for the period.
3. **For quarterly estimates.** Compute estimated taxable income, apply the owner's effective rate or safe-harbor basis, and show the estimated payment and due date. Label the tax figure an estimate, not filed advice.
4. **For year-end 1099s.** List contractors paid over the reporting threshold, flag missing W-9s/TINs, and total amounts per payee.
5. **Build the accountant packet.** Assemble P&L, category totals, reconciliations, asset purchases, and open questions in one organized handoff.
6. **List gaps.** Note missing receipts, uncategorized spend, and unanswered items.

This is preparation, not tax advice or filing. The owner and a licensed accountant review, approve, and file; do not submit payments or forms.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Job type / period / deadline.**
- **Income & expense summary by category.**
- **Quarterly:** estimated taxable income, estimate, due date (estimate only).
- **1099s:** payee, amount, W-9 status, threshold flag.
- **Accountant packet contents.**
- **Gaps & open questions (for owner/accountant).**
