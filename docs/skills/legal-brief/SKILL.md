## When to use
Use this when someone needs legal context fast: a daily digest of active matters, a focused briefing on a legal topic (a new regulation, a contract type, a jurisdiction), or a write-up of a legal incident. The goal is a decision-ready summary, not a memo of law-review depth.

## METHOD
1. **Fix the mode and audience.** Daily / Topic / Incident, and who reads it (exec, PM, ops). Tailor depth and jargon accordingly.
2. **Gather sources.** Active matters, relevant statutes/regs, contracts, prior guidance, and any incident facts. Note the date of each source.
3. **Extract what matters.** Pull only the facts, deadlines, obligations, and risks that affect the reader's decisions. Discard background they cannot act on.
4. **Separate law from opinion.** Mark settled requirements vs. interpretation vs. open questions.
5. **Surface deadlines and owners.** Any filing dates, response windows, or renewal triggers, each with a named owner.
6. **Flag escalation.** Note anything that needs licensed-attorney review before action.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Mode / audience / date.**
- **Bottom line:** 2-3 sentences.
- **Key points:** bulleted, each tagged [requirement] / [interpretation] / [open].
- **Deadlines & owners:** table.
- **Risks:** ranked, with exposure.
- **Escalate to counsel:** items requiring attorney review.
- **Sources:** with dates.
