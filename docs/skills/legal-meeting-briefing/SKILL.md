## When to use
Use this before a meeting with legal stakes — a negotiation, a regulator call, a board matter, a dispute discussion, a vendor sign-off — and after it to capture what was decided. It ensures the attendee walks in prepared and walks out with tracked commitments.

## METHOD
1. **State purpose and desired outcome.** What decision or agreement should exist when the meeting ends.
2. **Profile the parties.** Who attends, their interests, and their likely positions. Note any privilege or confidentiality boundaries.
3. **Assemble the record.** Relevant contracts, prior correspondence, open issues, and the current status of each. Flag any items still under attorney review.
4. **Prepare positions.** For each contested point: our position, acceptable range, fallback, and walk-away. Mark red lines.
5. **List questions to ask and answers to avoid** committing to without counsel.
6. **Capture outcomes.** During/after, record decisions, who committed to what, deadlines, and any items deferred to counsel.
7. **Log action items** with owner, due date, and status for follow-through.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Meeting / purpose / desired outcome.**
- **Attendees & interests.**
- **Key documents & status.**
- **Positions:** point, our stance, range, red line.
- **Questions to ask / commitments to defer.**
- **Decisions made** (post-meeting).
- **Action items:** owner, due date, status.
