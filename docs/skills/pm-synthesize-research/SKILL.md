# Synthesize Research

When to use: you have raw qualitative input, interview notes, survey responses, support tickets, sales call notes, and need to turn it into ranked themes with evidence, not a pile of quotes.

METHOD
1. Confirm the question: what decision does this research inform? Synthesis without a question produces trivia.
2. Code the raw data: tag each observation with the underlying need, pain, or behavior. Keep observations atomic and traceable to their source.
3. Cluster codes into themes. A theme is a recurring pattern, not a single loud voice. Note how many distinct sources support each.
4. Rank themes by frequency, severity of pain, and strategic relevance to the question. Separate signal (patterns) from anecdote (one person's strong opinion).
5. Attach evidence: 1-2 representative verbatim quotes per theme, plus counts. Flag where sample is thin or biased (e.g., only churned users).
6. Translate to implications: what each theme suggests you build, fix, or investigate, and confidence level.

OUTPUT FORMAT
- Research question and sources (counts, sample notes).
- Ranked themes: each with a one-line statement, support count, severity, and 1-2 quotes.
- Surprising findings or contradictions.
- Implications: recommended actions with confidence.
- Gaps: what the data cannot answer and what to research next.
Never inflate a single anecdote into a theme; mark low-sample findings as tentative.
