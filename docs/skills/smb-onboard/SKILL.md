When to use: Run this the first time a small-business owner arrives, or when they want to reset their setup. It gets them from zero to one real result and captures enough context to be useful going forward.

METHOD
1. Welcome and orient: explain in two sentences what the toolkit does and that the owner approves anything touching money or customers.
2. Connect the first two tools that matter most for this owner — typically accounting or payments plus a CRM or storefront. Confirm each connection works.
3. Run one quick recipe end to end (for example a sales brief or a month heads-up) so they see a real result immediately.
4. Interview the owner: business type, what they sell, busy seasons, biggest current headache, and goals. Keep it short and conversational.
5. Store the answers as durable business context so later skills can personalize.
6. Set a weekly check-in cadence the owner agrees to, and point them to smb-router for what to do next.

OUTPUT FORMAT
- Connection status for each of the two tools.
- The one recipe's result.
- A saved business-context summary the owner can correct.
- Confirmed weekly check-in day and a short "what's next" menu.
- No spend or customer contact happens during onboarding without approval.
