## When to use

Use this at month-end to close the books with confidence. It guides you step by step through reconciling recorded revenue against what payment processors actually deposited, flags mismatches, and assembles a packet for you or your accountant.

## METHOD

1. Gather the month's bookkeeping records and payout reports from each payment processor (card, online, marketplace).
2. Reconcile recorded income against processor deposits. Match by date and amount; identify unmatched transactions, fees not booked, refunds, and chargebacks.
3. Flag discrepancies with the likely cause and the dollar amount, ranked by size. Do not silently adjust the books.
4. Check for common gaps: uncategorized expenses, missing receipts, duplicate entries, and outstanding invoices.
5. Present findings and propose corrections. Apply only owner-approved adjustments, then export a clean review packet. Any change to financial records requires explicit approval.

## OUTPUT FORMAT

A reconciliation summary (recorded vs deposited, net difference). A ranked discrepancy list: item, amount, likely cause, suggested action. A gaps checklist. Then "Approve the adjustments to apply." Finish by exporting a month-end packet (reconciliation, flagged items, corrections log) and stating where it was saved. Books are the owner's to sign off.
