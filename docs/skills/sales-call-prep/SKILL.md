# Sales Call Prep

## When to use
Use this in the 15-30 minutes before a sales call when you need to walk in sharp: who is in the room, where the deal stands, what you must learn, and what a win looks like.

## METHOD
1. **Set the objective.** Name the single outcome that makes this call a success (advance to POC, secure economic buyer, unblock legal). Everything else serves it.
2. **Reload deal context.** Summarize stage, amount, timeline, last interaction, and open commitments from both sides.
3. **Research attendees.** For each name: role, likely priorities, and their probable stance (champion, skeptic, decision maker). Note anything personal or professional that builds rapport.
4. **Anticipate.** List their likely questions and objections, and your best answers with proof points.
5. **Build the agenda.** Time-boxed: opener, their priorities first, your discovery questions, value tie-back, then explicit next steps.
6. **Prepare discovery questions.** 3-5 open questions that surface pain, budget, authority, and timeline without interrogating.
7. **Define the ask.** Decide the specific commitment you will request before the call ends.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Objective:** one sentence.
- **Deal snapshot:** stage, value, timeline, last touch.
- **Attendees:** name — role — angle.
- **Agenda:** numbered, time-boxed.
- **Discovery questions:** bulleted.
- **Likely objections and responses:** paired.
- **The ask:** the close you will drive.
