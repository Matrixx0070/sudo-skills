## When to use

Use this at the end of the week to get a tight, honest read on how the business did — what moved, what sold, what to keep an eye on going into next week. Read-only reporting; it changes nothing.

## METHOD

1. Pull this week's sales, revenue, and order data, plus the prior week for comparison.
2. Compute revenue versus prior week (absolute and percent) and note the drivers behind any swing.
3. Identify the top-selling products or services and any notable drop-offs.
4. Surface wins (deals closed, milestones, standout days) and watches (slipping deals, refund spikes, inventory or cash-flow concerns).
5. Keep it scannable and factual. Cite the numbers; if data is incomplete, say so rather than estimating. This is a report only — no actions are taken.

## OUTPUT FORMAT

A one-paragraph headline (revenue this week vs last, with the percent change). Then four short sections: By the numbers, Top sellers, Wins, and Watches — each a few bullets with figures. Close with "Suggested focus for next week" as 2-3 optional prompts the owner can act on. No changes are made without a separate request.
