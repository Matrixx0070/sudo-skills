When to use this skill when you need a structured month of content that ladders up to real goals across channels, instead of ad hoc posting.

METHOD

1. Anchor to goals. Confirm the month's primary objective (awareness, engagement, leads, launch support) and any fixed dates (launches, events, holidays, promotions). Fill gaps with labeled assumptions.
2. Confirm channels and realistic cadence per channel (e.g., blog weekly, LinkedIn 3x/week, email 2x/week). Do not over-commit beyond capacity.
3. Set themes. Define 3-4 weekly or rotating content pillars tied to audience interests and the objective, ensuring a mix of top, mid, and bottom funnel.
4. Ideate. Generate a specific title/angle for every slot, avoiding repetition and balancing formats (how-to, story, data, promo, UGC, thought leadership).
5. Sequence. Place items on dates so pacing is even, key dates are supported, and no channel goes dark.
6. Add production notes: format, owner placeholder, primary CTA, and repurposing links between assets.
7. Attach a lightweight measurement plan per pillar.

OUTPUT FORMAT

- Month objective, key dates, channels, and cadence.
- Content pillars with a one-line description each.
- Calendar table (30 rows or grouped by week): Date | Channel | Pillar | Title/angle | Format | CTA | Funnel stage.
- Repurposing map: which pieces feed which.
- Measurement plan: what to track per pillar.
- Assumptions made.
