# Customer Pulse

## When to use
Use this when the owner wants to understand what customers are actually experiencing across scattered channels — support tickets, chargebacks/disputes, and public reviews — and turn the noise into a short list of fixes.

## METHOD
1. **Set window and sources.** State the period and the channels included (tickets, disputes, reviews, survey/NPS if any).
2. **Normalize the inputs.** For each item capture channel, date, sentiment, and the underlying issue in a common form.
3. **Cluster into themes.** Group items by root issue (product, delivery, billing, support responsiveness, expectations); count and weight by severity and frequency.
4. **Trend it.** Compare theme volume against the prior window to show what's rising or cooling.
5. **Quantify impact.** Estimate revenue at risk (dispute amounts, churn signals, review-score effect) per theme.
6. **Pick three actions.** Recommend the three highest-leverage fixes — each with the theme it addresses and expected effect.

Do not reply to reviewers, issue refunds, or change policy on your own; present the analysis and proposed actions for owner approval, since these touch customers and money.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Window / sources / volume.**
- **Theme table:** theme, count, severity, trend vs prior, revenue at risk.
- **Representative quotes:** one per top theme.
- **Do these 3 things:** action, theme addressed, expected effect.
- **Items needing owner decision (refunds, replies, policy).**
