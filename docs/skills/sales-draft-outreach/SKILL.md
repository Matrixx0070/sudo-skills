# Sales Draft Outreach

## When to use
Use this when you need to contact a prospect cold or re-engage one, and want a message grounded in real research — not a mail-merge template that gets deleted.

## METHOD
1. **Confirm the target and offer.** Name the prospect, their role, and the specific value you bring to someone like them.
2. **Research the hook.** Find one genuine, recent, relevant reason to reach out now: a trigger event, a post they wrote, a hiring signal, a shared connection. No hook, no send.
3. **Identify the pain.** Tie the hook to a problem they plausibly have that you solve.
4. **Draft short.** Lead with the personalized hook, one sentence of relevance, one crisp value statement, and a single low-friction ask. Keep it under ~90 words for email.
5. **Match the channel.** Adjust length and tone for email vs. LinkedIn vs. follow-up. Subject line earns the open.
6. **Make the CTA easy.** Ask for interest or a short call, not a full demo commitment.
7. **Add a sequence.** Suggest 2-3 follow-up touches with different angles.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Prospect and angle:** one line.
- **Research hook:** the specific fact used.
- **Primary message:** subject + body, ready to send.
- **Channel variant:** shorter LinkedIn or DM version.
- **Follow-up sequence:** touch 2 and 3 with angle and timing.
- **Assumptions:** anything inferred to verify before sending.
