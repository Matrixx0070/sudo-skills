# Margin Analyzer

## When to use
Use this when the owner suspects some products or services are carrying the business while others quietly lose money, or when considering a price change and wanting to see the effect before committing.

## METHOD
1. **List the offerings.** Enumerate the products/services in scope with unit price and units sold in the period.
2. **Assign direct costs.** For each, capture COGS or direct delivery cost (materials, labor, processor fees, shipping) per unit.
3. **Compute unit economics.** Calculate gross profit per unit, gross margin %, and total contribution by offering.
4. **Rank.** Sort by total contribution and by margin %; flag negative- and thin-margin items and hidden winners.
5. **Run pricing scenarios.** For selected items, model +/- price and cost changes; show the new margin and the break-even volume shift (how much volume you can lose before the change stops paying).
6. **Recommend.** Suggest price, bundle, or discontinue moves with the projected margin impact.

Do not change any prices; pricing and discontinuation decisions affect customers and revenue and are the owner's to approve.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Period / offerings covered.**
- **Unit economics table:** offering, price, unit cost, gross profit/unit, margin %, total contribution.
- **Ranking:** top contributors, thin/negative margin flags.
- **Pricing scenarios:** offering, change modeled, new margin, break-even shift.
- **Recommendations (for owner approval).**
