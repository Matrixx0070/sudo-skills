# Sales Competitive Intel

## When to use
Use this when a deal is competitive or a prospect names an alternative, and you need a battlecard: where you win, where you lose, how to reframe, and the traps to set.

## METHOD
1. **Scope the field.** Confirm your product and the specific competitors in play. A battlecard is per-competitor, not generic.
2. **Gather facts.** For each competitor collect positioning, target segment, pricing model, key features, integrations, and recent moves. Prefer verifiable sources; mark rumor as rumor.
3. **Map strengths and weaknesses** honestly — theirs and yours. Overstating your edge loses deals when the prospect catches it.
4. **Find the wedge.** Identify 2-3 differentiators that matter to this buyer's use case, not just any difference.
5. **Build the matrix.** Compare you vs. each competitor across the criteria the buyer actually weighs.
6. **Write objection handling.** For each "but competitor X does Y," give a truthful reframe and a discovery question that exposes the trade-off.
7. **Set landmines.** Questions the prospect should ask the competitor that surface their weaknesses.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Competitors covered:** list.
- **Comparison matrix:** rows = criteria, columns = us vs. each competitor.
- **Where we win / where we lose:** two honest lists.
- **Key differentiators:** 2-3, tied to buyer value.
- **Objection handling:** "they say" → "you say" pairs.
- **Landmines:** questions to plant.
- **Confidence:** verified vs. assumed sources.
