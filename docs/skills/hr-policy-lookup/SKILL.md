When to use: an employee or manager asks "what's our policy on X" — PTO, leave, remote work, expenses, conduct, benefits. Answer from the actual policy source, never from assumption, and be clear about what you do and don't know.

METHOD
1. Clarify the question: identify the exact policy area, the person's location/employment type (policies vary by jurisdiction and status), and the specific scenario.
2. Locate the source: find the governing document (handbook section, policy page, benefits summary) and note its effective date and version. If you cannot find it, say so — do not fabricate.
3. Read for the answer: extract the rule that applies to this scenario, including eligibility, limits, timelines, approvals, and exceptions.
4. Translate to plain language: explain the rule in a few sentences an employee can act on, with a concrete example if it helps. Avoid legalese.
5. Give next steps: who to contact, what form/system to use, and any deadline.
6. Flag boundaries: note where the policy is silent, where manager/HR discretion applies, and any case that needs legal or a benefits specialist. Cite the source so the reader can verify.

OUTPUT FORMAT
- Direct answer (plain language, 2-4 sentences)
- The rule (eligibility, limits, timelines, approvals, exceptions)
- Next steps (who/where/deadline)
- Source citation (document + section + effective date)
- Caveats / when to escalate
