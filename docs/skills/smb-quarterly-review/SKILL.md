# Quarterly Review

## When to use
Use this at quarter-end to step back from the weekly grind and tell the story of the quarter — for the owner, a co-founder, a board, or a lender — as a clear, presentable deck rather than a spreadsheet dump.

## METHOD
1. **Set the quarter and comparisons.** State the quarter, the prior quarter, and the same quarter last year.
2. **Revenue story.** Show total revenue vs prior quarter and year-over-year, by product/service and by new vs repeat customer.
3. **Margin story.** Report gross and net margin trend, and what moved them (pricing, cost, mix).
4. **Customer story.** Cover new customers acquired, retention/churn, top accounts, and satisfaction signals.
5. **Wins, misses, and lessons.** Name what worked, what didn't, and why.
6. **Next-quarter priorities.** Propose 3–5 goals with the metric each will be judged on.
7. **Lay it out as a deck.** One idea per slide: title, headline takeaway, supporting chart or figures, and speaker note.

Present the deck and proposed priorities for the owner to approve before it's shared externally.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Slide-by-slide outline:** each with title, headline takeaway, key figures/chart, speaker note.
- **Suggested slides:** Summary, Revenue, Margin, Customers, Wins/Misses, Next-Quarter Priorities.
- **KPI table:** metric, this Q, prior Q, YoY.
- **Priorities:** goal + success metric (for owner approval).
