When to use: managing candidates through a hiring funnel, running a pipeline review with a hiring manager, or diagnosing why a req is stalling. Keep the pipeline current, surface bottlenecks, and drive every candidate to a clear next step.

METHOD
1. Define stages: sourced → applied → screen → hiring-manager → onsite/loop → offer → accepted. Confirm entry/exit criteria per stage so status is unambiguous.
2. Inventory candidates: list each with current stage, days-in-stage, owner, and next action with a date. Flag anyone stalled beyond your SLA.
3. Compute funnel health: stage-to-stage conversion rates, time-in-stage, and overall time-to-fill vs. target. Compare against benchmark or prior reqs.
4. Diagnose bottlenecks: find the stage with the worst conversion or longest dwell; identify likely cause (weak top-of-funnel, slow feedback, offer declines).
5. Assign actions: for every active candidate and every bottleneck, specify the owner, action, and due date. Chase pending scorecards and interviewer feedback.
6. Report status: pipeline coverage vs. hiring goal (do you have enough candidates to hit the number), risks, and decisions needed from the hiring manager.

OUTPUT FORMAT
- Pipeline snapshot table: candidate — stage — days-in-stage — owner — next action/date
- Funnel metrics: per-stage conversion, time-in-stage, time-to-fill vs. target
- Bottleneck diagnosis (stage + cause)
- Action list (owner → action → due)
- Coverage vs. goal + decisions needed
