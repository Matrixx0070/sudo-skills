## When to use

Use this when you start the day and want a focused call list instead of a cluttered pipeline. It surfaces the five leads most likely to move, arms you with what to say, and reserves time to actually dial.

## METHOD

1. Pull open leads and deals from the CRM, including last-contact date, deal value, stage, and any recent activity (email replies, site visits, form fills).
2. Score each lead on freshness, intent signals, deal size, and stall risk. Prefer warm-but-cooling leads over cold ones.
3. Select the top five. For each, draft three talking points grounded in real history — never invent facts; if a detail is missing, say so.
4. Propose 2-3 calendar blocks that fit around existing meetings.
5. Confirm the blocks with the owner before writing anything to the calendar. Do not send emails or log outreach automatically.

## OUTPUT FORMAT

A ranked list of 5 leads: name, company, why-now score, last contact, and three bullet talking points each. Then a short "Suggested call blocks" section listing proposed times, followed by: "Approve these blocks and I'll add them to your calendar." Wait for a yes.
