This is general wellness information, not medical diagnosis or treatment. Consult a licensed healthcare professional for habits tied to a medical or mental-health condition (addiction, compulsive behavior, disordered eating).

## When to use

Use this when someone wants to build (or break) a wellness habit — movement, hydration, journaling, meditation, screen limits — and can name the behavior and roughly when it should happen.

**Not for:** substance-use disorders, self-harm behaviors, or compulsions requiring clinical treatment — refer to a professional.

## Method

1. Define the target behavior in one specific, tiny sentence (not "exercise more" but "do 5 push-ups after brushing teeth").
2. Anchor a cue. Decision point: prefer habit-stacking onto an existing routine ("after <existing habit>, I will <new habit>") over vague time-based cues.
3. Shrink the routine until it feels almost too easy — the goal is consistency first, scale later.
4. Attach a reward: something immediate and satisfying (a checkmark, a track, a small treat) so the loop closes.
5. Reduce friction for good habits (lay out gear, pre-fill the bottle); add friction for bad ones (remove the app, put the snack away).
6. Decision point: if you miss a day, apply the "never miss twice" rule — resume immediately rather than restarting from zero.
7. Track with a simple streak/checkbox and review weekly; once consistent for ~2-4 weeks, increase difficulty by one small step.

## Example

Goal: drink more water. Behavior: "after pouring morning coffee, fill a 1L bottle and put it on my desk." Cue: coffee (existing). Routine: fill bottle. Reward: tick a habit tracker. Friction cut: bottle stored by the coffee machine. Miss rule: never miss twice. Review Sundays; scale to 2L when 14-day streak hits.

## Pitfalls

- Starting too big; motivation fades and the streak dies in week one.
- Vague cues ("sometime in the evening") that never reliably trigger.
- No reward, so the brain never reinforces the loop.
- Treating one missed day as total failure and quitting, instead of resuming next day.

## Output format

```
HABIT: <tiny specific behavior>
CUE (stack): after <existing habit>, I will <new habit>
ROUTINE: <minimal version>
REWARD: <immediate reinforcement>
FRICTION: reduce <good-habit friction> / add <bad-habit friction>
MISS RULE: never miss twice

TRACKING: <streak/checkbox method> | REVIEW: weekly
SCALE-UP: after <n> days consistent, increase to <next step>
NOTE: General wellness info, not medical advice — see a professional for compulsive or condition-linked behaviors.
```
