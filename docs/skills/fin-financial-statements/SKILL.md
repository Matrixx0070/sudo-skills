# Financial Statements

## When to use
Use this to prepare or review the three primary financial statements for a period, to check that they articulate correctly, or to produce a management reporting package with variance commentary.

## METHOD
1. **Set the reporting basis.** State the entity, period, currency, and basis (GAAP / IFRS / management), and the comparison columns (prior period, prior year, budget).
2. **Build the income statement.** Revenue, COGS, gross profit, operating expenses by category, operating income, other income/expense, taxes, net income; compute margins.
3. **Build the balance sheet.** Assets (current, non-current), liabilities (current, non-current), equity; confirm assets = liabilities + equity.
4. **Build the cash flow statement.** Operating (indirect method from net income plus non-cash and working-capital changes), investing, financing; sum to net change in cash.
5. **Tie the statements together.** Net income flows to retained earnings; ending cash equals the balance-sheet cash line; PP&E and debt roll forward.
6. **Run variance analysis.** For each material line, compute change vs comparison in dollars and percent; flag items past the materiality threshold.
7. **Write commentary.** Explain each flagged variance with its driver.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Entity / period / basis / comparison columns.**
- **Income statement:** line, current, comparison, $ var, % var, margin.
- **Balance sheet:** with A = L + E check shown.
- **Cash flow statement:** three sections, net change, ending cash tie-out.
- **Articulation checks:** net income → equity, ending cash match (pass/fail).
- **Variance table:** line, variance, driver.
- **Commentary and flags.**
