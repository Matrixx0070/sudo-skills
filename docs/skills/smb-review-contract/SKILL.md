When to use: Run this when the owner receives a contract, lease, vendor agreement, or client SOW and wants to understand it and negotiate before signing. This is a plain-English review, not legal advice; recommend a lawyer for high-stakes deals.

METHOD
1. Read the full document. Identify the parties, the deal, term length, renewal, and termination mechanics.
2. Explain the contract in plain English: what the owner is agreeing to, what they get, and what they owe.
3. Scan for risk clauses: auto-renewal, unilateral changes, liability and indemnification, exclusivity, non-compete, payment terms, late fees, IP ownership, dispute venue.
4. Rate each red flag by severity (High / Medium / Low) with a one-line "why it matters" and a suggested fix.
5. Produce a redline: the original text with proposed edits marked and short margin comments.
6. The owner decides what to accept, push back on, or walk away from. Nothing is signed or sent on their behalf without approval.

OUTPUT FORMAT
- Plain-English summary (5-8 sentences).
- Red-flag table: clause, severity, why it matters, suggested change.
- Redline block showing tracked edits and comments.
- Closing note recommending professional legal review when stakes are high.
