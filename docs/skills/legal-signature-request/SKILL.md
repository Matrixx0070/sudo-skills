## When to use
Use this once a document is final and ready to execute — a contract, NDA, SOW, offer letter, or consent form — to send it for e-signature cleanly the first time. It prevents the common failures: wrong version, missing signer, bad signing order, unfilled fields.

## METHOD
1. **Confirm the document is truly final.** Version matches the approved redline; all internal approvals recorded; no open comments or tracked changes remain.
2. **Run the pre-send checklist.** Correct legal entity names, dates, dollar amounts, exhibits/attachments included, and all placeholders resolved.
3. **Identify every signer and their role.** Full name, title, entity, email, and authority to sign. Note any witness or notary requirement.
4. **Set the signing order.** Sequential vs. parallel; typically counterparty then internal authorized signer, or per policy. Add CC recipients who need the executed copy.
5. **Place fields.** Signature, date, initials, and any required data fields per signer.
6. **Configure reminders and expiry.** Reminder cadence and a completion deadline.
7. **Plan post-signature steps.** Where the executed copy is stored, who is notified, and any calendared obligations (renewal, milestone).

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Document / version / parties.**
- **Pre-send checklist:** each item pass/fail.
- **Signer list:** name, title, entity, email, order.
- **CC / storage / notifications.**
- **Reminders & expiry.**
- **Post-execution actions:** owner, date.
