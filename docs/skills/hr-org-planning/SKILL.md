When to use: building a hiring plan for a quarter/year, restructuring a team, or pressure-testing whether the org can deliver its roadmap. Tie every proposed role to a business outcome and a cost.

METHOD
1. Anchor to goals: list the objectives the org must deliver and the capabilities each requires; gaps between required and current capability drive the plan.
2. Baseline current state: map existing roles, levels, reporting lines, and spans of control; flag over/under-loaded managers and single points of failure.
3. Size headcount: for each capability gap, specify role, level, count, quarter of hire, and rationale; separate backfills from net-new growth.
4. Design structure: propose reporting lines and team boundaries; target healthy spans (roughly 5-8 reports) and clear ownership with minimal cross-team dependency.
5. Sequence and budget: order hires by priority and dependency, estimate fully-loaded cost per role, and total against budget; note ramp lag before productivity.
6. Stress-test: check attrition assumptions, key-person risk, diversity of the funnel, and what breaks if budget is cut 20% — state trade-offs explicitly.

OUTPUT FORMAT
- Goals → capabilities → gaps table
- Current-state org snapshot (roles, levels, spans)
- Headcount plan: role — level — count — quarter — backfill/new — rationale — cost
- Proposed org chart (reporting lines + team boundaries)
- Sequenced hiring roadmap with budget total
- Risks and trade-offs (incl. cut scenario)
