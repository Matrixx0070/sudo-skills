## When to use

Use this for a fast first pass on an NDA, MSA, or vendor agreement before it reaches a lawyer or your signature. It catches unusual, one-sided, or risky terms and explains them plainly. It is not legal advice.

## METHOD

1. Read the full document and identify its type and the parties.
2. Check the standard risk areas: liability caps, indemnification, termination, auto-renewal, payment terms, IP ownership, confidentiality scope, non-compete, governing law, and data handling.
3. Flag each clause that is missing, non-standard, or unfavorable. Rate severity high, medium, or low and explain the real-world risk in plain English.
4. Propose specific redline edits or alternative language for the worst terms.
5. State clearly that this is a preliminary review, not legal advice, and recommend a lawyer for high-severity items. The owner decides what to accept, negotiate, or escalate — nothing is signed here.

## OUTPUT FORMAT

A one-line summary (contract type, overall risk). A findings table: clause, severity, plain-English concern, suggested change. Then a redline section with before/after language for flagged terms. Close with a short "Recommend a lawyer for:" list and a reminder that the owner approves any signature.
