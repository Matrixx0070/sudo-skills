## When to use

Use this when you're ready to hire and want a complete, consistent packet instead of piecing it together. It produces the job post, the interview structure that keeps evaluations fair, and an offer letter template.

## METHOD

1. Gather the role basics from the owner: title, responsibilities, must-have and nice-to-have skills, location or remote, and pay range.
2. Draft an inclusive job post — role summary, responsibilities, qualifications, what you offer, and a clear application step. Avoid biased or exclusionary language.
3. Build a structured interview guide: consistent questions mapped to each competency, with a 1-5 scoring rubric and behavioral anchors per level.
4. Draft an offer letter template with placeholders for name, title, compensation, start date, and contingencies — flagged as requiring legal review before use.
5. Present all three for the owner's review. Nothing is posted or sent to a candidate; the owner approves and distributes.

## OUTPUT FORMAT

Three clearly labeled sections: Job Post (ready to publish), Interview Guide (questions plus rubric table), and Offer Letter Template (with placeholders and a "have counsel review" note). End with "Review and approve before posting or sending to any candidate."
