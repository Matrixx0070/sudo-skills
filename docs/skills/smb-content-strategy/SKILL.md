# Content Strategy

## When to use
Use this when you need a data-backed plan for what to promote next month — not finished posts. It reads your sales history and turns it into a prioritized brief that a creator (or smb-canva-creator) can execute later.

**Not for:** producing designs, captions, or a posting calendar (hand the brief to smb-canva-creator), or spending on ads. This plans; it does not create or publish.

## Method
1. Pull sales data for the trailing 3-12 months: revenue, units, and margin by product or service.
2. Identify top performers worth amplifying and slow movers that may need a push, a bundle, or retirement. Decision point: weight by margin, not just volume — a high-revenue, low-margin item may not deserve promotion.
3. Detect seasonality — recurring peaks and troughs — and flag what typically sells in the coming 30 days.
4. Map findings to content themes and a rough cadence, prioritizing high-margin winners and timely seasonal plays.
5. Present the brief for owner approval. This skill plans only; it does not create designs, schedule posts, or spend on ads.

## Example
Trailing 6 months: "Deep clean" package is 34% of revenue at 55% margin — top performer to amplify. "Gift cards" spiked every year in the two weeks before Mother's Day (3 weeks out) — seasonal play. "One-room touch-up" is high volume, 12% margin — candidate for a bundle, not a solo promo. Brief prioritizes: (1) Deep clean testimonials, (2) Mother's Day gift-card push, (3) touch-up + deep-clean bundle.

## Pitfalls
- **Chasing revenue over margin.** Promoting your lowest-margin bestseller can grow sales and shrink profit.
- **Missing the seasonal window.** A brief that lands after the peak is worthless — check lead time to the event.
- **Vague themes.** "Post more about quality" isn't actionable; tie every theme to a specific product and number.
- **Drifting into production.** Stop at the brief; designs and scheduling are a separate, approved step.

## Output format
```
Findings (numbers cited):
  Top performers: <item> — rev $__, margin __%
  Slow movers: <item> — <push/bundle/retire>
  Seasonal notes: <what sells in next 30 days, when>

30-day content brief (prioritized):
  1. Theme — rationale (data) — target product — formats — cadence
  [...]
"Approve this brief to move into production."
```
