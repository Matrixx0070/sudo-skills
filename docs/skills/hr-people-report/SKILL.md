When to use: preparing a monthly/quarterly people review, answering a leadership question about the workforce, or tracking a specific metric like regretted attrition. Report numbers accurately, define every metric, and lead with insight over data dumps.

METHOD
1. Scope the report: audience, period, and org cut (company/function/team). State the as-of date and the data source.
2. Headcount: report starting/ending headcount, hires, exits, net change, and open reqs; break down by function and level.
3. Attrition: compute annualized attrition = exits / average headcount, and split voluntary vs. involuntary and regretted vs. non-regretted. Show trend vs. prior periods.
4. Diversity: report representation by relevant dimensions at overall, leadership, and hiring-funnel stages; compare to prior period. Handle small-n groups carefully to protect privacy — suppress or aggregate.
5. Org health: fold in span/layers, tenure distribution, internal mobility, and engagement/eNPS if available.
6. Interpret: call out the 3-5 signals that matter, likely drivers, and recommended actions. Distinguish correlation from cause; flag data-quality caveats.

OUTPUT FORMAT
- Executive summary (top 3-5 insights + recommended actions)
- Headcount section (table + net-change waterfall)
- Attrition section (rate, voluntary/regretted split, trend)
- Diversity section (representation by level/funnel, period delta)
- Org-health metrics (spans, layers, tenure, mobility)
- Metric definitions and data caveats
