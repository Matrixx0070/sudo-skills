## When to use
Use this to answer routine, recurring legal questions — "can we use this logo," "is this clause standard," "what's our data-retention period," "can I share this with a partner" — where an approved template exists. It resolves the common case quickly and routes anything unusual to a licensed attorney.

## METHOD
1. **Classify the inquiry.** Match it to a known category (IP use, data handling, contract clause, employment, disclosure, third-party sharing). If no category fits, escalate.
2. **Run escalation checks first.** Flag to counsel if the matter involves litigation, a regulator, a novel fact pattern, dollar exposure above threshold, personal legal advice, or a non-standard jurisdiction. Do not template these.
3. **Select the approved template** for the category and confirm it is current.
4. **Fill it with the specific facts.** Replace every placeholder; never leave generic language that misstates the situation.
5. **Add caveats.** State assumptions and the boundary of the answer ("this covers X, not Y").
6. **Mark disclaimer and review need.** Note whether attorney review is required before sending.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
- **Inquiry** restated + category.
- **Escalation check:** pass / escalate (with reason).
- **Response draft:** templated, facts filled.
- **Assumptions & scope limits.**
- **Attorney review:** required / not required.
- **Suggested next step** for the requester.
